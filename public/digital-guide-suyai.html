Beach & Surf

Beaches BINGIN AREA

  • Bingin Beach
    White-sand cove framed by cliffs with a famous left-hand reef break, cliffside warungs, and a buzzy sunset scene.
    End of Jl. Pantai Bingin, Pecatu · long stairway from clifftop parking
  • Dreamland Beach
    Wide, easily accessed white-sand beach with punchy beach-break waves suitable for intermediates and big sunset crowds.
    Easy access
    Inside Pecatu Indah Resort, off Jl. Raya Uluwatu
  • Impossible Beach
    Long reef-strewn stretch between Bingin and Padang Padang with shallow left-hand reef waves best left to experienced surfers.
    Off Jl. Pantai Impossible, Pecatu
  • Cemongkak Beach
    Quiet, low-key cove between Bingin and Dreamland with dramatic cliffs, powerful waves, and one of the area's least-crowded sunsets.
    Off Jl. Pantai Cemongkak, Pecatu
  • Labuan Sait Beach
    Local name for Padang Padang Beach — a small white-sand cove reached through a limestone crevice, with world-class surf offshore.
    Jl. Labuan Sait, Pecatu · stairs through cliff cave

Beaches ULUWATU AREA

  • Padang Padang Beach
    Compact white-sand beach reached via a famous limestone-cave staircase, home to the legendary "Balinese Pipeline" left.
    Jl. Labuan Sait, Pecatu · 120 steps through cliff cave
  • Thomas Beach
    Quiet, low-key white-sand bay reached by a long stairway; calmer water makes it one of the better swimming spots in Uluwatu.
    Off Jl. Labuan Sait, Pecatu · approx. 160 steps down
  • Blue Point Beach
    Local nickname for the Suluban/Uluwatu beach area below Blue Point cliffs — dramatic cave entry and the Uluwatu surf break right there.
    Jl. Pantai Suluban, Pecatu · clifftop stairs through warungs
  • Suluban Beach
    Hidden cave beach reached by ducking under limestone cliffs; legendary sunsets, cliffside bars, and the iconic Uluwatu surf lineup.
    Jl. Pantai Suluban, Pecatu · steps down past Single Fin
  • Nyang Nyang Beach
    Long, almost-empty 2 km stretch of white sand reached by a steep 500+ step hike — wild, remote, and rarely crowded.
    Off Jl. Nyang-Nyang, Pecatu · long descent on foot

Beaches UNGASAN AREA

  • Melasti Beach
    Dramatic limestone-cleaved access road leads to a wide white-sand beach with day clubs, swimming-friendly water and big sunsets.
    Easy access
    Jl. Melasti, Ungasan · paved cliff road straight to sand
  • Green Bowl Beach
    Secluded white-sand cove at the bottom of around 300 steps; quiet swimming, snorkelling, and a reef break offshore.
    End of Jl. Bali Cliff, Ungasan
  • Pandawa Beach
    Long family-friendly white-sand beach with gentle waves safe for swimming, sunbeds, warungs, and a dramatic cliff-cut entry road.
    Easy accessKids
    Jl. Pantai Pandawa, Kutuh
  • Karma Beach
    Private white-sand beach reached by inclinator from Karma Kandara resort — calm swimming water, beach club service, day-pass entry.
    Easy access
    Inside Karma Kandara, Jl. Villa Kandara, Ungasan
  • Gunung Payung Beach
    Hidden white-sand cove reached via a rock tunnel and 280 steps; calm clear water great for swimming away from crowds.
    Off Jl. Pura Gunung Payung, Kutuh · past Bali National Golf
